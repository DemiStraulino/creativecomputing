On a starr’d night

Prince Lucifer uprose. 
Tired of his dark dominion swung

the fiend 





Above the rolling ball in cloud part screen’d, 

Where sinners hugg’d

their spectre of repose. 

Poor prey to

his hot fit of pride were those. 







And now upon his western wing he lean’d, 

Now his huge

bulk o’er Afric’s sands careen’d, 

Now the black planet shadow’d

Arctic snows. 





Soaring

through wider zones that prick’d his scars 

With memory of

the old revolt from Awe, 

He reach’d a middle height, and at the

stars, 









Which are the

brain of heaven, he

look’d, and

sank. 

Around the ancient track march’d, rank on rank, 

The army of

unalterable

law.
